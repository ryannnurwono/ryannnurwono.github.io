--- layout: misc title: Irony ---

Irony - the expression of one's meaning by using language that normally signifies the opposite, typically for humorous or emphatic effect

Example:

"'I stopped him,' she said, staring at the place where the fence used to be. 'I took and put my babies where they'd be safe' - Beloved


Types of Irony


  • Verbal Irony - The speaker intends to be understood as meaning something that contrasts with the literal or usual meaning of what he says
  • Situational Irony - when the opposite of what we expect to happen, happens
  • Dramatic Irony - irony that is inherent in speeches or a situation of a drama and is understood by the audience but not grasped by the characters in the play


Purpose of Irony in Literature & Poetry

  • Emphasize a central idea
  • Provide a contrast between expectations and reality
  • Add depth of thought to the story
  • Help us relate to and understand the situation better
